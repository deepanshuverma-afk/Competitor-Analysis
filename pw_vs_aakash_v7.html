Executive Overview
PW vs Aakash — Top-line performance at combined center level · AY26 YTD
🏆
Volume Leadership — PW +12%

PW leads with 8,516 more admissions. Bihar, UP, East, Maharashtra and Rajasthan are PW strongholds. Patna alone: PW 5,628 vs Aakash 1,344.

⚠️
ARPU Deficit — ₹14,878 Gap

Aakash charges ₹14,878 more per student. In JEE/NEET it widens to ₹21–26K. This is PW's biggest revenue leakage despite volume leadership.

🚨
Critical White Spots

South India (Chennai, TN, Andhra) — Aakash dominates with high ARPU of ₹90K+. Kolkata — PW leads with 3,579 admissions vs Aakash's 2,019, though Aakash commands a higher ARPU (₹83K vs ₹62K).

📊
Foundation — PW Gap is Closable

PW has 27,758 Foundation admissions vs Aakash's 31,107 — a gap of just 3,349 (~11%). This is PW's most competitive segment and the gap can be closed.

💪
Pathshala Model — Volume Win

PW's Pathshala (11,566 adm) vastly outscales Aakash's equivalent (6,257 adm) — +85% more admissions. But ARPU gap is ₹19K — pricing needs fixing.

🎯
Golden Centers — ARPU Gap ₹18K

PW's own Golden Centers average ₹60K ARPU vs Aakash's ₹78K — a ₹18K per student gap in PW's best centers. Pricing revision is critical here.
